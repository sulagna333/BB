Plant profile

Money Plant

Indoor · Low light · Trailing vine

About this plant

Money Plant (Pothos / Epipremnum aureum) is one of the most popular and beginner-friendly indoor plants. It grows long trailing vines that can climb, hang, or spread across shelves — perfect for decorating rooms.

It survives low light, irregular watering, and adapts to almost any indoor environment. Easy to propagate in water or soil, making it a great plant to share with friends.

Light

Low to medium indirect light. Avoid harsh direct sunlight.

Water

Water when top soil dries. Overwatering causes yellow leaves.

Soil

Any well-draining indoor mix. Thrives in cocopeat + compost.

Pet safety

Mildly toxic if chewed by pets. Keep out of reach.

Seasonal behavior

  • Summer: Faster growth; water slightly more often.
  • Monsoon: Soil stays wet longer; reduce watering.
  • Winter: Growth slows; water sparingly.

Quick info

  • Scientific name: Epipremnum aureum
  • Type: Indoor trailing vine
  • Care level: Very easy
  • Ideal spot: Study desk, shelf, low-light corners
  • Growth speed: Fast
  • Length: 3–10 feet vines
