Plant profile

Tulsi (Holy Basil)

Balcony · Medicinal · Sacred herb

About this plant

Tulsi (Holy Basil) is one of the most important medicinal and spiritual plants in Indian homes. It grows well outdoors on balconies, courtyards, or sunny windows where it receives good airflow.

Tulsi loves sunlight and regular watering. With proper care, it grows into a bushy plant that provides aromatic leaves for tea, remedies, and daily rituals. It is low-maintenance as long as it gets sun and is not overwatered.

Light

Requires 4–6 hours of sunlight daily. Best on a balcony.

Water

Water when top soil feels dry. Avoid waterlogging.

Soil

Light, airy soil with compost. Ensure good drainage.

Pet safety

Safe. Tulsi is edible and medicinal.

Seasonal behavior

  • Summer: Thrives with regular watering; avoid harsh mid-day sun for young plants.
  • Monsoon: Reduce watering; risk of fungal infections increases.
  • Winter: Growth slows; bring indoors if nights get too cold.

Quick info

  • Scientific name: Ocimum tenuiflorum
  • Type: Medicinal herb
  • Care level: Easy
  • Ideal spot: Sunny balcony / near window
  • Growth speed: Fast
  • Height: 1–2.5 feet
