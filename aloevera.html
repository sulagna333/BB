Plant profile

Aloe Vera

Succulent · Sunny window · Low maintenance

About this plant

Aloe Vera is a hardy succulent known for its thick leaves filled with cooling, healing gel. It thrives in bright sunlight and needs very little water, making it perfect for balconies, sunny windows, and beginners.

Aloe grows best in slightly dry conditions with plenty of airflow. Overwatering is the biggest risk — the plant stores water inside its leaves, so it doesn’t need frequent watering.

Light

Strong sunlight. Ideal for sunny windows or balconies.

Water

Water every 10–15 days. Let the soil dry completely before watering again.

Soil

Fast-draining mix: cactus soil + sand + perlite.

Pet safety

Mildly toxic to pets if eaten. Keep out of reach.

Seasonal behavior

  • Summer: Grows well, but avoid afternoon sun for young plants.
  • Monsoon: Soil stays wet longer — cut watering to avoid rot.
  • Winter: Water once every 3 weeks; growth slows.

Quick info

  • Scientific name: Aloe barbadensis miller
  • Type: Succulent
  • Care level: Easy
  • Ideal spot: South-facing window / sunny balcony
  • Growth speed: Medium
  • Height: 1–2 feet
